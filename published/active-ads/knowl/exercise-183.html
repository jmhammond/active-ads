Exercise 3.
Suppose \(0 \lt k \lt n\text{.}\) Define the \(\binom{n}{k}\)-tree to be the binary tree with \(\binom{n}{k}\) at its root and left and right subtrees having roots \(\binom{n-1}{k-1}\) and \(\binom{n-1}{k}\text{,}\) respectively. If \(k=0\) or \(k=n\text{,}\) then the \(\binom{n}{k}\)-tree is a leaf containing the number 1.
  1. Draw the \(\binom{5}{1}\) and \(\binom{5}{2}\) trees.
  2. How many leaves does a \(\binom{n}{k}\)-tree contain? Prove your answer.
