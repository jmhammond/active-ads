Consider the subgroup \(C = \{00000, 01101, 11110, 10011\}\) of the group \(S=\mathbb{Z}_2^5\text{.}\) Suppose your answer to a multiple choice question with four possible responses is stored in five bits of computer memory using one of the four elements of \(C\text{.}\) Now suppose the computer is hacked but we know that no more than one of the five bits in your response has been changed. Can your response be recovered?
