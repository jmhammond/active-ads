Question 24.1.1. Response Question.

(adopteds from [1]) Suppose we treat addition as logical or and multiplication as logical and. Furthermore, suppose that \(B\) stands for banana. Then to say you could have as many as two bananas, we could write \(B^0+B^1+B^2=1+B+B^2\text{.}\) Suppose that in addition you could have up to three apples (use \(A\) for apples) and zero or one pears (use \(P\) for pears). What algebraic expression represents all your choices in selecting fruits? Identify the part of this expression where you select exactly two pieces of fruit.
