Exercise 1.
  1. Construct the truth table for \(x= (p \land \neg q) \lor (r \land p)\text{.}\)
  2. Find an example other than \(x\) itself of a proposition generated by \(p\text{,}\) \(q\text{,}\) and \(r\) that is equivalent to \(x\text{.}\)
  3. Find an example of a proposition that is not equivalent to \(x\) or a contradiction that implies \(x\text{.}\)
  4. Find an example of a proposition that is not equivalent to \(x\) or a tautology that is implied by \(x\text{.}\)
